How to Approve/Reject Channels

  1. Log in and go to the moderate link.
  2. Go to the Unreviewed Channels (it is possible to do this in Firefox or Miro).
  3. Add the first channel (the channels that have been waiting longest are at top). You can optionally click the "Subscribe to all 10 channels" link at the top of the page to save time.
  4. Now it's time to kick the channel's tires:
    1. Does the feed work? If you get an error, just choose "No" and then click the "Broken" button from the Standard Rejections. The channel manager will get a helpful e.mail with information and links.
    2. Does the media obviously violate copyright? You are not a copyright cop. However, if you see full episodes of the Simpsons, and they aren't coming from Fox's website (hulu.com), the channel probably isn't legit. If something is unclear, approve it and the copyright holder can file a DMCA request. If you click the reject for "Copyright" button, the channel manager will be contacted and have a chance to respond.
      The explicit stuff and spam is the most subjective and difficult to deal with. We will eventually have an user based solution (i.e. Miro users will decide what they think is inappropriate or explicit, and get warnings based on what other users do). However, for the time being, we make the call. Our rule of thumb is that if a feed would get an X rating (esp. if it involves explicit sex), we don't allow it in the guide. Also, if the channel looks like pure spam you can reject it. If you're not comfortable making this judgement, just click the "Don't Know" button. Otherwise, choose "Explicit" from the Standard Rejections. The channel manager will be contacted and have a chance to respond.
    3. If you see a channel that is all audio files (.mp3 or similar), just click the "Audio" button.
    4. If you find other problems, such as video formats that don't play back, you can make a custom rejection. It will send the channel manager an email that they can respond to.
    5. If you are ever not sure what to do, simply click the "Don't Know" button and let someone else help you make the decision.
    6. If the channel made it through all the tests, you can Approve it! The channel manager will automatically receive a congratulatory email.
  5. Rinse and repeat.

Communicating with Channel Managers

Any time you reject a feed or add a message to the "Conversation with Channel Manager" (this is at the bottom of every channel page), an email message is sent to the manager. They will get your note and be able to respond.

Whenever a channel manager adds a message or replies in the conversation, their channel will appear in the "Channels waiting for Reply" section. The channels in this section should be treated as high priority.

When a channel is "Waiting for Reply", you simply reply to the message (or re-reject the channel, if it is still not approvable) and the channel will fall out of the "Waiting for Reply" section.

Communicating with Fellow Moderators

There is a very basic bulletin board built into the channel guide. The latest posts show up on the main moderation page, in order to see the history and/or post your own message, click the (see all posts) link.

IRC Channel: #miro (irc.freenode.net).

You can access the channel with a traditional client, or through this web interface. Remember, just type anyone's full nickname into a conversation to get their attention.

GetSatisfaction is where a lot of support for Miro happens. If you're able to join in and answer a few unanswered questions, that is excellent.

Team Miro Mailing List: this list is intended for volunteers, Miro enthusiasts, and anyone else who is excited about getting involved.

Sharing Favorite Channels with Moderators

Just click the "Share with Moderators" button and the channel will be available on the main moderation control panel. If you approve a channel that seems interesting or appealing, definitely share the love.

Dealing with "Don't Know" Channels

If you see something in here, you should basically treat it as an "unapproved channel" that someone else wasn't sure how to deal with. If you're a more experienced moderator, you should treat these as higher priority. Use the same guidelines for approving it, as outlined above.

What is Super Moderator Status?

There are two levels of moderator -- Moderator and Super Moderator. They are very similar, except that Super Mods can feature channels.

Super Mod status is pretty powerful — we promote people who have: been around the community for a while, proven themselves to be a reliable moderator, and are a good judge of channels (ie. share your favorites!).

Reporting Miro Guide Bugs and Suggesting Features

If you find something that seems buggy or wrong, you can add a ticket to the channel guide bug tracker.

If you have a great idea, please submit it as a feature request; please keep in mind that we run a tight ship and must very carefully consider each new feature.
